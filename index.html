Expertise Across All Restaurant Types

We understand the unique real estate needs of every restaurant category. Whether you're a national chain or a local favorite, we can secure the perfect site for your concept.

Quick-Service Restaurants (QSR) / Fast Food

High-traffic locations with excellent visibility and easy access are key. We specialize in securing sites with strong demographics and drive-thru potential.

Fast-Casual

Combining convenience with a higher-quality experience, these concepts thrive in mixed-use areas with strong daytime populations. We target sites that attract both lunch and dinner crowds.

Casual Dining

These full-service restaurants require locations with strong evening and weekend traffic. We focus on sites in established retail corridors and growing residential communities.

Family-Style

Specialty and Themed

The Advantages of a Ground Lease

Partnering with us provides more than just capital savings. It's a strategic move for long-term success.

Massive Capital Preservation

Deploy your funds into your operations, marketing, and team—not into a non-income-producing land purchase. This is the single most effective way to improve your startup's financial health.

Access to Prime Locations

High-traffic, desirable locations are often not for sale or are prohibitively expensive. Our expertise and capital allow us to secure these premium sites for your franchise.

Improved Financial Metrics

Leasing land instead of owning it can improve your return on investment (ROI) and other key financial ratios. This makes your business more attractive to lenders and future investors.

Simplified Real Estate Management

We handle the complexities of land ownership. You avoid dealing with zoning issues, property taxes on the land, and other associated burdens, allowing you to focus purely on your business operations.

Long-Term, Stable Tenancy

Our ground leases are structured for the long term (e.g., 20-99 years), providing you with the site security you need to invest confidently in building and growing your franchise for decades to come.

Flexible and Transferable

A ground lease is a valuable asset. If you decide to sell your franchise, the lease can typically be transferred to the new owner, making your business an even more attractive acquisition.
